The Pacific Ocean is the largest and deepest of Earth's oceanic divisions. It extends from the Arctic Ocean in the north to the Southern Ocean in the south and is bounded by Asia and Australia in the west and the Americas in the east.

Amazing Ocean Facts

Water World

The oceans hold about 96.5% of all Earth's water. The remaining 3.5% is freshwater found in glaciers, ice caps, and groundwater.

Deepest Point

The Mariana Trench in the Pacific Ocean is the deepest part of the world's oceans, reaching about 11,034 meters (36,200 feet) deep.

Biodiversity

Scientists estimate that there may be between 700,000 and 1 million species in the oceans, with about two-thirds still undescribed.
